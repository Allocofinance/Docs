1\. Purpose and Scope

This methodology explains the NAV calculation framework for ETP / ETF-style products on the Alloco platform. NAV is a core reference for issuance, redemption, secondary market anchoring, RFQ pricing, risk control, disclosure reporting and exception handling.

The methodology may apply to crypto-native products, TradFi-linked products, thematic baskets, quantitative strategy products, RWA exposures and other structured products. Each product may define specific parameters based on underlying assets, market hours, custody structure, data availability and legal requirements.

2\. Three-Layer NAV System

| NAV Type | Main Use | Update Frequency | Core Feature |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Official NAV | Formal settlement, primary create / redeem, daily report, audit and investor reporting. | Usually daily or after the product settlement cycle. | Highest accuracy; based on full holdings, cash, fees, liabilities and corporate-action checks. |
| iNAV | Secondary market price anchoring, RFQ quote reference and premium / discount monitoring. | May update every 15-60 seconds during active markets. | More real-time; depends on data feeds, order books, TWAP, position snapshots and cash estimates. |
| Risk NAV | Risk control, suspension, quote protection, redemption limits and emergency mode. | Real-time or event-driven. | More conservative; includes stale price, liquidity, custody, counterparty and closed-market haircuts. |

3\. Core Calculation Formula

Total NAV is generally calculated as:

Total NAV = asset market value + cash + receivables - liabilities - accrued fees - cost reserves - risk discounts

NAV per Token is generally calculated as:

NAV per Token = Total NAV / Total Token Supply

Asset value may include spot assets, equities, ETFs, derivatives, perpetual-contract margin, swap exposure, stablecoins, treasury bills or cash. Liabilities may include pending redemptions, accrued fees, trading costs, financing costs, taxes, custody expenses, audit fees or other product-level obligations.

4\. Data Inputs and Price Sources

| Data Type | Examples | Recommended Validation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Price data | Primary feed, secondary feed, TWAP, broker quote. | Multi-source deviation checks; if thresholds are exceeded, enter Degraded state. |
| Position data | Onchain balance, CEX position, broker statement. | Compare with previous snapshot; abnormal changes require manual or multisig confirmation. |
| Cash and payables | USDC, USD, pending trades, fees. | Onchain balance checks and reconciliation with bank / custodian / administrator reports. |
| Corporate actions / events | Dividends, splits, halts, mergers, liquidations. | Match event calendars; restrict real-time create / redeem if processing is incomplete. |
| Risk parameters | Haircut, status, confidence. | Generated by rules engine; material changes should be recorded with approval logs. |

5\. Official NAV and iNAV Process

Official NAV is used for formal settlement and reporting. The process may include locking the valuation time, collecting prices / positions / cash / fees / liabilities / corporate actions, reconciling onchain supply and custody records, calculating assets and liabilities, applying adjustments, generating a NAV report, and publishing summary fields or report hash to the product page or onchain records.

iNAV is an intraday or active-market reference used for secondary market anchoring and RFQ quotes. It prioritizes tradability, explainability and timely updates smaller than audit-level completeness. It may use bid / ask mid, 60-second TWAP, multi-exchange median price, broker quote or other reliable sources.

6\. Risk NAV and Haircut Model

| Haircut Type | Trigger Condition | Example Range |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Liquidity Haircut | Order book thin, AMM depth declines or underlying liquidity deteriorates. | 0-100 bps or higher. |
| Stale Price Haircut | Price update exceeds defined freshness threshold. | 10-100 bps. |
| Closed-Market Haircut | Underlying market is closed, on holiday or not executable. | Product-specific. |
| Counterparty Haircut | Custodian, broker, CEX or swap counterparty risk rises. | 10-100 bps. |
| Event Haircut | Halt, major announcement, corporate action, onchain attack or stablecoin depeg. | Case-by-case. |
| FX / Settlement Haircut | Cross-currency, cross-market or cross-time-zone settlement uncertainty. | 10-100 bps. |

Risk NAV may be lower than iNAV or Official NAV. Its purpose is to protect users, the product asset pool and system stability during uncertain states.

7\. NAV State Machine

| State | Meaning | Allowed Actions | Restrictions |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Fresh | Data is current, price sources normal and holdings reconciled. | Normal trading, RFQ, create / redeem. | Regular limits and fees. |
| Stale | NAV or key price update is delayed. | Small trades and limited RFQ. | Restrict large create / redeem and widen spread. |
| Frozen | Underlying market is closed or halted. Official NAV is used. | Small trades or view-only, depending on product. | Suspend large instant create / redeem. |
| Degraded | Data-source conflict, lower-liquidity or abnormal price condition. | Limited trading and limited redemption. | Increase haircut and suspend high-risk paths. |
| Emergency | Oracle failure, custody event, extreme deviation or major security event. | Usually view-only or redeem-only. | Suspend secondary trading, RFQ and creation. |

8\. Pricing Use Across Flows

| Scenario | Recommended Pricing Reference | Protection Logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Small secondary buy / sell | DEX price versus iNAV. | Display premium / discount and slippage. |
| Medium RFQ buy | iNAV + spread + execution cost. | Multiple quotes and best execution priority. |
| Medium RFQ sell | iNAV - spread - execution cost. | Quote based on inventory, market state and risk fees. |
| Large create | Official NAV or execution-adjusted NAV. | Settle after underlying execution to avoid dilution of existing users. |
| Large redeem | Official NAV after execution cost. | T+1 / T+N or batch settlement to avoid run dynamics and stale NAV arbitrage. |
| Exception state | Risk NAV or suspension. | Protect asset pool and long-term holders. |

The purpose is to separate a specific product’s underlying assets, cash, receivables, payables and rights / obligations from the Alloco operating company and other products.

- The SPV / SPC or asset pool may hold or arrange to hold underlying assets, cash and related rights.

- The product token acts as an operational record and transfer rail. Its legal nature depends on product documents and user eligibility.

5\. Onchain and Offchain Custody

Onchain assets

Onchain assets may be held through vault contracts, multisig wallets, MPC custody wallets, compliant wallets or smart contract structures. Controls should address transparency, permissions, upgrade safety and asset security.

- Vault contracts should limit mint / burn, asset transfer, fee collection and emergency pause permissions.
- Multisig or MPC wallets should use signing thresholds, operation logs, address whitelists and amount limits.
- Major upgrades should use timelock, multisig approval or guardian mechanisms.
- Onchain balances should be reconciled with NAV reports, product supply and user share records.

Offchain assets

Equities, ETFs, cash, treasury bills, broker positions, swap exposure or other traditional assets may be recorded and managed through custodians, brokers, administrators or SPV / SPC asset pools.

When underlying assets trade only during specific market hours, onchain products may still show or offer limited interaction, but execution, NAV updates and large create / redeem flows should respect market hours, stale-price risk and risk-control rules.

8\. Permissions and Operational Controls

Custody and operating permissions should follow least-privilege principles. Material operations should require approval, logs and review.

- Role separation across product operation, NAV publication, risk control, asset transfer and compliance approval.
- Single-transaction, daily, product-level and address-level limits.
- Whitelist controls for custody accounts, brokers, APs, MMs and critical contracts.
- Alerts for abnormal transfers, NAV deviation, redemption spikes and price-source outages.
- Audit logs recording operator, time, reason, approver, transaction hash and report link.
