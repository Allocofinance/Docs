Alloco is designed so that product assets are held separately from Alloco's operating assets and from the assets of other products. This page explains how custody is structured across onchain and offchain asset types, how segregation is maintained, and what controls govern permissions, reconciliation, and wind-down.

<Note>
Alloco does not guarantee the solvency, security, availability, or performance of any third-party custodian, broker, bank, exchange, protocol, or counterparty. Review the product-specific custody description before investing.
</Note>

## Purpose and Core Principle

The purpose of this document is to help you, issuers, partners, and reviewers understand how product assets may be held, how they may be separated from operating-company assets, how they correspond to product shares, and how your interests may be protected during exceptions.

**Core principle:** Product assets should be recorded separately from operating-company assets, other product assets, and service-provider proprietary assets wherever possible. You should rely on legal structure, custody records, NAV reports, and reconciliation processes — not only on platform statements.

| Asset / Exposure Type | Possible Holding Method | Main Records |
|---|---|---|
| Onchain assets | Smart contract vault, multisig wallet, or compliant custody wallet. | Onchain balance, transaction hash, and vault records. |
| Stablecoins / cash | Vault, custody account, bank account, or issuer account. | Onchain balance, bank/custodian report, and cash ledger. |

## Legal Wrapper (SPV / SPC)

Alloco-related products may be supported by SPVs, SPCs, segregated portfolios, fund entities, or other applicable legal vehicles. The purpose is to separate a specific product's underlying assets, cash, receivables, payables, and rights/obligations from the Alloco operating company and other products.

- The operating company may provide technology, front end, protocol, data, product operation, or service support.
- The SPV/SPC or asset pool may hold or arrange to hold underlying assets, cash, and related rights.
- Custodians, brokers, administrators, managers, or other service providers may provide custody, execution, valuation, and reporting support.
- The product token acts as an operational record and transfer rail. Its legal nature depends on product documents and your eligibility.

## Onchain Asset Custody

Onchain assets may be held through vault contracts, multisig wallets, MPC custody wallets, compliant wallets, or smart contract structures. Controls address transparency, permissions, upgrade safety, and asset security:

- Vault contracts limit mint/burn, asset transfer, fee collection, and emergency pause permissions.
- Multisig or MPC wallets use signing thresholds, operation logs, address whitelists, and amount limits.
- Major upgrades use timelock, multisig approval, or guardian mechanisms.
- Onchain balances are reconciled with NAV reports, product supply, and user share records.

## Offchain Custody

Equities, ETFs, cash, treasury bills, broker positions, swap exposure, or other traditional assets may be recorded and managed through custodians, brokers, administrators, or SPV/SPC asset pools.

When underlying assets trade only during specific market hours, onchain products may still display or offer limited interaction — but execution, NAV updates, and large create/redeem flows respect market hours, stale-price risk, and risk-control rules.

Assets held offchain are typically recorded in the fund's name through a custodian or brokerage arrangement, not directly in your name.

## Asset Types and Custody Structures

| Holding Method | Description |
|---|---|
| Direct holding | The asset pool directly holds stablecoins, tokens, equities, ETFs, or cash. |
| Indirect holding | The asset pool obtains economic exposure through a fund, SPV, note, swap, synthetic tool, or custody arrangement. |
| Margin holding | Strategy products may maintain margin and hedge positions at an exchange, broker, or prime broker. |
| Cash buffer | A product may retain cash or short-term liquidity assets for fees, redemptions, slippage, and exception states. |
| Risk reserve | Certain products may maintain cost reserves or risk reserves for trading costs, closed-market risk, counterparty risk, or redemption processing. |

## Segregation and Ledger Records

| Record Category | Content | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Asset records | Underlying assets, cash, stablecoins, margin, and receivables. | Calculate NAV and verify the asset pool. |
| Liability records | Payable redemptions, fees, transaction costs, taxes, and management fees. | Avoid overstating net assets. |
| Share records | Token supply, user shares, whitelist, and locked shares. | Subscription, redemption, transfer, and user-rights recordkeeping. |
| Transaction records | Subscriptions, redemptions, trades, rebalances, RFQ, and broker execution. | Audit trail and exception replay. |
| Risk records | NAV state, haircut, exception event, and suspension record. | Risk control and user disclosure. |

## Reconciliation and Controls

Alloco maintains layered reconciliation requirements, including:

- Onchain supply versus vault records.
- Vault balance versus blockchain data.
- Broker holdings versus internal ledger.
- Custodian report versus NAV report.
- Cash balance versus pending subscriptions/redemptions.
- Strategy positions versus CEX/broker/perp account statements.
- Fees versus product rules.
- reportHash versus original NAV report.

Custody and operating permissions follow least-privilege principles. Material operations require approval, logs, and review:

- Multisig or MPC controls for large asset transfers, contract upgrades, and emergency pauses.
- Role separation across product operation, NAV publication, risk control, asset transfer, and compliance approval.
- Single-transaction, daily, product-level, and address-level limits.
- Whitelist controls for custody accounts, brokers, APs, MMs, and critical contracts.
- Alerts for abnormal transfers, NAV deviation, redemption spikes, and price-source outages.
- Audit logs recording operator, time, reason, approver, transaction hash, and report link.

## Third-Party Service Providers

| Service Provider | Possible Responsibilities | Main Risks |
|---|---|---|
| Custodian | Hold securities, cash, stablecoins, or other assets. | Bankruptcy, freeze, operational error, or legal dispute. |
| Broker / PB | Execute equities, ETFs, derivatives, or financing trades. | Order rejection, delay, margin change, or settlement failure. |
| Exchange / CEX | Execute crypto trades, perpetual contracts, or funding-rate strategies. | Withdrawal limits, bankruptcy, hacking, or liquidation risk. |
| Market maker / AP | Quote, hold inventory, create/redeem, and repair premium/discount. | Insufficient inventory, quote withdrawal, or performance failure. |
| Oracle / data source | Provide prices, market state, and NAV inputs. | Delay, error, manipulation, or outage. |
| Administrator / manager | Support reporting, registration, reconciliation, and wind-down. | Operational error, delay, or file inconsistency. |

## Subscription, Redemption and Reconciliation Flow

Custody arrangements cover the complete asset flow for subscriptions and redemptions, including:

1. Receipt of USDC/cash or other subscription assets.
2. Execution of underlying assets or strategies.
3. NAV calculation after execution and fees.
4. Minting or recording product shares.
5. Locking or burning tokens during redemption.
6. Preparing settlement assets.
7. Updating product ledgers.

## Wind-Down Procedures

Alloco maintains wind-down procedures for product termination. Products predefine exception and wind-down paths for:

- Custodian, broker, exchange, or stablecoin events.
- Inability to value or trade underlying assets.
- Regulatory restrictions.
- Uneconomic product size.
- Smart contract, key, or infrastructure incidents.
- Redemption pressure.

Wind-down may include suspending new creation, stopping secondary routing, selling or settling underlying assets, deducting fees and liabilities, distributing remaining assets to eligible holders, and publishing final reports.

Custody-related disclosure may include the product asset-pool structure, main custody or brokerage arrangements, asset-segregation principles, vault or contract addresses, recent NAV reports and reportHash, holding range or summary exposure, cash buffer, pending redemptions, fees, and exception/suspension/wind-down notices.
