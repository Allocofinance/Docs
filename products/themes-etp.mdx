## How themes become products

Alloco's **Theme to Product Engine** converts a macro narrative into a structured, investable ETP through a defined pipeline:

1. **Narrative** — the theme is articulated as a clear investment thesis with a defined scope and time horizon
2. **Blueprint** — the thesis is translated into a product blueprint that identifies eligible asset classes, geographies, and instruments
3. **Basket rules** — the blueprint becomes a set of enforceable rules covering asset selection, weighting methodology, concentration limits, and rebalancing triggers
4. **Issued ETP** — the basket is wrapped onchain with published NAV, full disclosure documentation, and access through DEX, RFQ, or the primary market

Every step of this process is documented in the product's prospectus so you can evaluate exactly how the theme was translated into the basket you are buying.

## Rebalancing and maintenance

Themes ETPs follow a **weekly settlement and rebalance cycle**. The basket rules define:

- **When the product rebalances** — triggered by the weekly cycle and any rules-based conditions such as asset drift, eligibility changes, or index reconstitution events
- **How assets are selected and weighted** — objective criteria set in the basket rules, not discretionary judgment
- **What happens when an asset becomes ineligible** — substitution or removal rules are defined in the product prospectus

Secondary market trading is available continuously via DEX or RFQ. The market price may differ from NAV between rebalancing windows.
