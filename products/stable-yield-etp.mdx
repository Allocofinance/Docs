Stable Yield ETPs are structured yield strategies wrapped onchain, designed to pursue more consistent return profiles than pure directional or thematic products. Each product defines its yield sources, risk limits, and rebalancing logic upfront — so you can evaluate exactly how the strategy works and what risks it carries before you invest. The name 'Stable' describes the product's design objective, not a guaranteed outcome; your capital is not protected, and yield is not fixed or assured.

## Yield sources

Stable Yield ETPs may draw on one or more of the following yield sources depending on the product's strategy:

- **Onchain yield** — staking rewards, liquidity provision premia, funding rate capture, basis carry, or other crypto-native yield mechanisms
- **TradFi-linked yield** — exposure to traditional fixed-income instruments such as bonds, treasury bills, or interest rate products accessed through broker API, custody account, or structured wrapper
- **Combination strategies** — some products blend onchain and TradFi-linked yield sources to target a more diversified return profile, with defined rules for how each source is weighted and rebalanced

Each product's prospectus identifies the specific yield sources, how they are accessed, and the risks associated with each.

## How yield is managed

Stable Yield ETPs use a rules-based approach to manage yield generation and control risk:

- **Rules-based rebalancing** — the product's weighting across yield sources is reviewed and adjusted on a weekly settlement cycle according to defined rules; there is no discretionary override
- **Risk NAV as a buffer** — Risk NAV incorporates haircuts for liquidity, stale prices, counterparty exposure, and closed-market conditions, providing a more conservative valuation reference that protects the asset pool during uncertain conditions
- **Weekly settlement** — positions are settled weekly; NAV is calculated and published after each cycle, with iNAV updating continuously during active markets
- **Suspension mechanisms** — if underlying yield sources become impaired, illiquid, or cannot be valued, the product's risk-state logic may restrict trading or creation/redemption to protect existing holders

## Access

To access a Stable Yield ETP, you need a compatible wallet and must complete KYC verification in line with the product's eligibility requirements. Once eligible, you can invest through:

- **DEX** — swap for smaller orders with visible slippage and premium/discount relative to iNAV
- **RFQ** — request a firm quote for medium-sized orders based on iNAV, spread, and current execution cost
- **Primary market** — create or redeem units at Official NAV for large orders, subject to whitelist eligibility, market hours, and product-specific conditions
