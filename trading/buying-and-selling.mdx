You can access Alloco ETP units through three routes depending on your order size and eligibility. Whether you're swapping quickly through a DEX, requesting quotes from market makers, or participating in the primary market as an institution, this page walks through each path.

## Choose your access route

| Order Type | Route | Best For | Key Consideration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small orders | DEX / Instant Swap | Quick access | Slippage and premium/discount visible at execution |
| Medium orders | RFQ (Request for Quote) | Reduced slippage | Multiple quotes available; wallet and KYC may be required |
| Large orders | Primary Create / Redeem | NAV-based pricing | Eligibility requirements apply; subject to settlement cycle |

---

## Buying via DEX

For small orders, the fastest way to buy ETP units is through a direct on-chain swap on the Alloco app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your wallet">
    Go to [app.allocofi.com](https://app.allocofi.com) and connect your wallet. Supported wallet types are listed on the connection screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete KYC if required">
    Some products require identity verification before you can trade. Check the product page to confirm whether KYC is needed before you proceed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the ETP from the product catalogue">
    Browse the product catalogue and open the ETP you want to buy. Review the product description, underlying assets, fees, and risk disclosures.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Swap stablecoin or token for ETP units">
    Enter the amount you want to swap. The interface will display the estimated output, current slippage, and any premium or discount relative to iNAV.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the transaction">
    Review the transaction details and confirm in your wallet. The market price at execution may differ from the NAV displayed at the time you initiated the swap.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Buying via RFQ

For medium-sized orders, the RFQ (Request for Quote) route lets you collect competitive quotes from market makers before committing to a trade. This can reduce slippage compared to a direct DEX swap.

1. **Submit a quote request** — specify the ETP and the amount you want to buy or sell.
2. **Receive quotes** — one or more market makers respond with executable prices. Quotes are based on the current iNAV, execution cost, inventory, and market conditions.
3. **Review and confirm** — compare the quotes, select the best one, and confirm execution. Quotes are time-limited, so confirm promptly before they expire.

KYC and wallet verification may be required before you can access the RFQ flow. Check the product page for eligibility details.

## Primary market subscription

The primary market is available to large users, institutions, and authorized participants (APs). Instead of buying on the secondary market, you submit a creation request directly — new ETP units are minted in exchange for the underlying assets or an accepted subscription currency.

Key points to understand before subscribing:

- **KYC and whitelist** — you must complete full KYC and be approved for primary market access before submitting a request.
- **Dealing days** — creation requests are only processed on scheduled dealing days defined in the product documents.
- **Settlement cycle** — settlement follows the product's defined cycle (T+1, T+N, or weekly), not instant execution.
- **Creation fee** — a creation fee applies to primary market transactions. See the product's fee schedule for the exact rate.
- **Minimum amounts** — most products set a minimum creation size. Check the product documents for the threshold that applies.

Contact the Alloco team or refer to the product prospectus if you want to apply for primary market access.

## Selling and redeeming

**Secondary market sale** — you can sell ETP units at any time through the DEX or RFQ routes described above. The sale price reflects the current market price, which may be above or below NAV.

**Primary market redemption** — eligible users and authorized participants may redeem ETP units directly through the primary market. Redemptions are governed by the terms in the product documents and may be subject to:

- Minimum redemption amounts
- Cut-off times and designated dealing days
- Notice periods before the redemption is processed
- Settlement periods (T+1, T+N, or product-specific cycle)
- Redemption fees
- Gates or limits on the total redemption volume per period
- Suspension rights if underlying markets are closed or conditions are extreme

Review the product's prospectus and redemption terms carefully before submitting a redemption request.

<Warning>
  Submitting a subscription or redemption request does not guarantee settlement at the NAV displayed at the time of submission. Requests may be delayed, partially filled, or rejected due to compliance review, insufficient funding, blockchain congestion, smart contract failure, market closure, trading suspension, or liquidity constraints.
</Warning>
