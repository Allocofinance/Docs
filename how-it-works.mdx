Alloco is onchain ETP issuance and access infrastructure. Every product on the platform follows a defined lifecycle: a market idea or investment theme is structured into a rules-based product blueprint, issued through a dedicated legal and onchain structure, continuously valued using a three-layer NAV system, and made accessible to eligible users through multiple trading and redemption paths. Understanding this lifecycle helps you know exactly what you are accessing, how your assets are held, and how the product you hold is priced at any given moment.

## The product lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Theme or idea → product blueprint">
    Every Alloco product starts with a market narrative, investment theme, professional insight, or quantitative strategy. That idea is formalised into a structured product blueprint that defines the product's ticker and name, eligible underlying assets, investment rules and rebalancing logic, fee schedule, NAV methodology and data sources, risk limits, and subscription and redemption conditions. Nothing is left to interpretation — the rules are written down before any capital enters the product.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Product issuance">
    Once the blueprint is finalised, the product is issued through a dedicated legal and onchain structure. A separate fund, SPV, or segregated portfolio is established to hold the underlying assets. Onchain share tokens are then minted to represent your economic interest in that fund. Your tokens track the performance of the underlying portfolio — but you do not directly own or control the underlying assets themselves; the fund holds them on your behalf.
  </Step>
  <Step title="NAV and pricing">
    Throughout the product's life, Alloco's three-layer NAV system continuously calculates the value of every product. The **Official NAV** is used for formal settlement and primary market transactions. The **iNAV** (indicative NAV) updates in near real time during active markets to anchor secondary trading and RFQ pricing. The **Risk NAV** applies conservative haircuts and triggers suspension logic when market conditions deteriorate. Together, these three layers ensure that the price you see reflects the most accurate, up-to-date valuation available.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access and trading">
    You can access Alloco products through three routes depending on your order size and eligibility. **Secondary market trading** via a DEX or swap interface suits most everyday purchases and sales. **RFQ (Request for Quote)** routes connect you to market makers for medium-sized orders, potentially reducing slippage. **Primary market creation and redemption** is available to large users, institutions, and authorised participants, allowing direct subscription and redemption at or near Official NAV — subject to KYC, whitelist approval, and product-specific conditions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key platform modules

Every Alloco product is built on six core modules working together.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Product Engine" icon="gear" href="/products/overview">
    Defines the product's name, ticker, objective, eligible users, fee schedule, minimum units, risk limits, rebalancing rules, and subscription/redemption cycles. The Product Engine is the rulebook that governs everything the product does.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Vault / Share Token" icon="vault" href="/trading/buying-and-selling">
    Records product shares and tracks subscription and redemption status onchain. Your share token represents your economic interest in the product's underlying fund. It is not an unconditional ownership claim or a guarantee of capital unless explicitly stated in the product documents.
  </Card>
  <Card title="NAV Engine" icon="calculator" href="/trading/nav-and-pricing">
    Calculates Official NAV, iNAV, and Risk NAV by combining asset values, cash balances, liabilities, accrued fees, unsettled trades, and applicable haircuts. The NAV Engine is the source of truth for product valuation across all trading and settlement workflows.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Liquidity Layer" icon="arrow-right-arrow-left" href="/trading/buying-and-selling">
    Routes your orders across DEX pools, RFQ market makers, authorised participants, broker APIs, and primary create/redeem flows depending on your order size, eligibility, and current market conditions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Legal Wrapper" icon="file-contract" href="/disclosures/custody-overview">
    Each product uses an SPV, SPC, segregated portfolio, or equivalent legal structure to separate the product's assets, your economic rights, and the Alloco operating company's own assets. This separation is a key protection for users.

  <Card title="Disclosure Layer" icon="book-open" href="/disclosures/risk-disclosures">

## How assets are held

Alloco products use a fund structure that keeps your assets separate from both the platform and other products.

When you invest in an Alloco ETP, here is what actually happens:

1. **A dedicated fund or legal entity** (SPV, SPC, or segregated portfolio) is established for that specific product
2. **The fund purchases and holds the underlying assets** — equities, tokens, stablecoins, derivatives positions, or other instruments — in its own name, through custodians or brokers
3. **You hold fund shares** (ETP units), represented by your onchain share tokens, that track the performance of the fund's underlying portfolio
4. **You do not directly own, hold, or control the underlying assets** — the fund holds them on your behalf, and your rights are defined by the product documents and applicable law

This structure is designed to protect you. The fund's assets are segregated from Alloco's operating assets, meaning that operational issues at the platform level do not automatically affect the underlying product assets.

<Note>
  Alloco is technology and product infrastructure. It is not always the issuer of every ETP available on the platform. The specific issuing entity, fund structure, and legal jurisdiction vary by product. Always refer to the relevant product prospectus, custody overview, and offering documents to understand who the issuer is and how your assets are held.
</Note>
