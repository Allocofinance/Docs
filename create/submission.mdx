To get started with creating an ETP on Alloco, you submit your idea through the platform or via email. Alloco's team then reviews the submission for compliance, feasibility, and structural fit. This page explains what to include in your submission, what you represent when you submit, and what happens next.

## How to submit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your submission">
    Work through the checklist below and gather the key details about your idea. You don't need a complete, polished document — a clear description of your thesis and strategy is enough to begin the conversation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit via the platform or email">
    Visit [app.allocofi.com](https://app.allocofi.com) to use the submission form, or email your idea directly to [support@allocofi.com](mailto:support@allocofi.com). Include as much detail from the checklist as you have available.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Alloco reviews and follows up">
    Alloco's team will review your submission and contact you with questions, feedback, or next steps. Review timelines vary depending on the complexity of the proposed product.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Submission checklist

Include as much of the following as possible in your initial submission:

- **Market thesis or investment idea** — what market insight drives this product and why it's investable
- **Target asset universe** — which assets, sectors, or exposures the product would reference
- **Strategy rules** — how the portfolio is constructed, weighted, and managed
- **Proposed rebalancing frequency** — daily, weekly, monthly, event-driven, or other
- **Performance history** — any track record or backtest data relevant to the strategy; this must be accurate and fully supported by underlying data
- **Your professional background** — relevant for professional issuer products; include your role, institution, and credentials
- **Intended target investor audience** — retail, professional, institutional, or a specific eligible investor type
- **Preferred product type** — crypto-native, TradFi-linked, thematic, structured-risk, or professional issuer

<Tip>
  You don't need to specify the legal structure or disclosure format in your submission. Alloco will work through those details with you during the review process if your idea is approved for development.
</Tip>

## Creator representations

By submitting a product idea, you represent that:

- **You have the right to submit the strategy** — the idea is yours to propose and you are not acting on behalf of someone else without authorization
- **It does not infringe third-party rights** — the strategy, name, and any materials you include do not violate anyone else's intellectual property or contractual rights
- **It is not misleading** — the strategy description, objective, and any market context you provide are accurate and not designed to deceive
- **All performance data is accurate** — any performance history, backtests, or simulations you include are genuine, complete, and fairly presented
- **Your submission complies with applicable law** — you are permitted to make this submission under the laws and regulations that apply to you

## After submission

Alloco reviews each submission for compliance and product feasibility. If your idea passes initial review, Alloco will reach out to discuss the product in more detail. If the product moves forward, a commercial agreement — covering any fees, revenue-sharing, licensing, or other commercial terms — is negotiated separately before launch.

Review and development timelines vary depending on:

- The complexity of the underlying strategy
- The legal structure required
- The target investor and jurisdiction
- Data availability and valuation methodology

You will not be obligated to proceed, and Alloco is not obligated to launch a product, simply because a submission was made.

<Warning>
  Submitting a product idea grants Alloco no right to use your intellectual property beyond the review process without a separate written agreement. That said, generic market ideas — a sector thesis, a macro view, a widely discussed theme — are not proprietary in themselves. IP protections apply to specific, documented strategies: defined rules, original methodology, and documented processes that are distinctly yours.
</Warning>
